Microsurgery & Reconstructive Surgery

Traumatic hand injuries due to use of domestic machinery and industrial machinery are rising. These injuries often very severe can lead to loss of digits, part of a digit and in some cases severe disability. The aim and principle of microsurgery and reconstructive microsurgery is to restore form, function and sensation by utilizing microsurgery techniques for repair of nerve, blood vessel, tendons and soft tissue. The following are surgical procedures,

  • Flap cover for upper and lower limbs
  • Finger reconstruction
  • Toe to thumb transfer
  • Joint transfer
  • Replantation surgery
  • Tendon transfer / reconstruction

Arthroscopy Procedures

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure that uses an arthroscope (camera) to diagnose and treat joint conditions. Specialised instruments are inserted through small incisions to perform procedures such as ligament repair, cartilage treatment. Arthroscopy causes less disruption to surrounding tissues, leading to reduced pain, minimal scarring, and faster recovery compared to open surgery. The return to physical activities depends on the type of procedure performed and the joint involved, with minor procedures allowing quicker recovery and complex surgeries requiring structured rehabilitation.

Wrist Arthroscopy

  • Arthroscopic TFCC repair
  • Synovectomy
  • Ligament reconstruction
  • Arthroscopy assisted fracture reduction
  • Ganglion cyst excision

Shoulder Arthroscopy
